<h4><strong>School History</strong></h4>
<p>Poi Ching School was founded in 1919 by the late Reverend Kwok Koh Mou
(郭可模) as a Chinese-medium school on Victoria Street. In 1924, facing financial
difficulties, the school came under the management of the Futsing Association
and was subsequently relocated to the Association’s office on Queen Street.</p>
<p>In 1958, Poi Ching School became a government-aided institution. The school
later moved from Queen Street to Toa Payoh in 1970. In 1992, it was selected
by the Ministry of Education as a Special Assistance Plan (SAP) school,
offering both English and Chinese as first languages.</p>
<p>Poi Ching School relocated once again in 2000 to its current premises
at Tampines Street 71. The school launched its Bi-Cultural Programme in
2008. In 2014, it completed the PERI upgrading project, which included
the construction of a new seven-storey teaching block. The following year,
Poi Ching School transitioned to a single-session model and established
a Student Care Centre to better support working parents.</p>
<p>In 2016, the school was accorded the Learning for Life Programme, “D’ARTS@PCS.”</p>
<p>In 2020, Poi Ching School was recognised with the Applied Learning Programme
– the BEST Programme (Developing a Bilingual, Effective Speaker and WriTer
in Every PCS Student). That same year, the school celebrated its 100th
anniversary.</p>

<br>OUR SCHOOL CREST
<br>校徽的浅解</p>
<p>校徽的整个含义总结成以下四句：
<br>书中乾坤大，
<br>笔下天地宽，
<br>少具鸿鹄志，
<br>大作栋梁材。</p>
<p>书 - 代表学无止境，学问博大精神。
<br>Book - Symbolises lifelong learning.</p>
<p>笔 - 求得学问， 他的笔下自然是天地。
<br>Pen - One who seeks and finds knowledge will have the world under one's
feet.</p>
<p>海上升起一轮红日 - 孩子如旭日初升，他小时候立下大志好好求学，长大后做一个有用的人材。
<br>Rising sun emerging from the sea - The resolution of a child to diligently
seek knowledge and learn will lead to contributing and concerned citizen.</p>
